install

macOS & Linux — one step $ curl -fsSL https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ayush-sharaf/rumba/master/install.sh | bash

That's it. The installer grabs the right prebuilt binary for your machine, verifies its checksum, and installs mpv, yt-dlp, and ffmpeg for you (via Homebrew on macOS, or apt/dnf/pacman on Linux). Then just run rumba — it reads your logged-in YouTube Music session from your browser. (Building from source? See the README.)

features

♪ Full playback

Search, library, liked, playlists, artists & albums. Endless radio, queue, lyrics, like/dislike — driven by mpv.

⌁ Native & tiny

Pure Rust + ratatui. One ~9 MB binary, no Electron, no browser engine. Audio-only — never downloads video.

⚿ No setup auth

Reads your browser's YouTube Music session directly — no Google Cloud project, no copy-pasting tokens.

⇄ Multi-account

Signed into several Google accounts? Pick the right one at login or switch live with c.

⌨ Configurable keys

Vim-style by default; remap anything in ~/.config/rumba/config.toml.

◷ Light on resources

~25 MB RAM vs 400–500 MB for the web tab. See the numbers below.
